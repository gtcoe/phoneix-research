Quality Power's strategic genius is occupying the highest-value, lowest-competition layer of the power equipment sector. An 800kV HVDC reactor requires decades of engineering expertise, PGCIL/ABB qualification, special winding technology (CTC magnet wire), and testing infrastructure costing hundreds of crores. There are fewer than 5 manufacturers globally who can supply this. Quality Power is the only Indian company in this club. This is a genuine moat — not just a temporary advantage.

Quality Power is now a multi-entity power technology group, not just a single manufacturer. Understanding each entity separately is essential because they serve different markets, have different margins, and contribute differently to consolidated numbers.

The Sangli plant — the 9x capacity expansion explained
!
Currently: ~₹150–200 Cr standalone revenue at near-full utilisation. The Sangli plant (HVDC coils, reactors) has been running at maximum capacity for years. This is why standalone growth has been ~40–60% despite massive global demand — they simply can't take more orders than the plant can produce.
+
Post-expansion (June 2026): 9x capacity = ₹1,500–2,000 Cr potential standalone revenue. The 320,000 sq ft facility being built at Sangli will house a 2,500 kV AC high-power test lab — one of the largest globally — and a magnet wire manufacturing line. This unlocks the ability to take orders that Quality Power currently has to decline. The capacity expansion is the single most important catalyst for the entire investment thesis.
+
First HVDC CTC Magnet Wire facility in India (Q3 FY27): Currently HVDC-grade magnet wire is imported (limited global suppliers, price volatile). Making it in India = lower cost + faster delivery + proprietary supply chain. This is backward integration that will further widen the margin moat vs any competitor attempting to enter the HVDC market.

Risks — what can go wrong at this valuation and scale
⚠️ The 3x problem: At ₹9,835 Cr mcap, 3x needs ₹29,505 Cr
This is the most important fact for Garvit's portfolio. Quality Power is already at ₹9,835 Cr market cap — 10x larger than when listed in Feb 2025 at ~₹850 Cr (post-issue market cap). The stock has already had its 10x re-rating. Now generating 3x from ₹9,835 Cr requires either: (a) PAT growing from ~₹165 Cr to ₹500+ Cr by FY28 while maintaining 25x PE = ₹12,500 Cr mcap = still only 1.3x, OR (b) PAT ₹600–800 Cr by FY28–29 at 30–40x PE = ₹18,000–32,000 Cr = 1.8–3.3x. 3x is possible but requires extraordinary execution over FY27–FY29. This is an accumulated-profit stock, not a discovery opportunity.
1
PE of 60–93x on trailing earnings — any hiccup triggers significant de-rating.
Quality Power is priced like a tech company, not an industrial manufacturer. ABB India trades at 70x, Siemens India at 50x. Quality Power at 60–93x is in that league. But Quality Power is far smaller (₹9,835 Cr vs ABB India at ₹85,000 Cr) and carries execution risk that established MNCs don't. A missed quarter, capex delay, or order cancellation could send the stock -30 to -40% instantly at this PE level. This is not hypothetical — the stock already corrected from ₹1,082 to ₹630 (-42%) within months of listing before recovering.
2
Debtor days rising from 113 → 149 — working capital stress building.
In project-based engineering businesses, debtor days rising means customers are delaying payments. At ₹775 Cr revenue and 149 debtor days, Quality Power has ~₹316 Cr locked in receivables. If this rises further (which is possible in an export-heavy business with longer payment terms), it creates cash flow stress despite strong PAT. The company needs to maintain this or it will erode the "near-zero net debt" advantage gained from the IPO. Monitor this every quarter — it's the most important leading indicator of financial health.
3
Sangli 9x expansion is high-stakes execution — any delay hits FY27 revenue.
The FY27 bull case depends almost entirely on the Sangli plant commissioning by June 2026 and ramping to meaningful utilisation by Q3–Q4 FY27. A 320,000 sq ft facility with a 2,500 kV test lab is a complex engineering project. The test lab itself requires specialised equipment with 12–18 month lead times. If commissioning slips to Q3 FY27 and test lab qualification takes another 6 months, meaningful HVDC coil production from Sangli doesn't start until FY28. This single delay could defer the "9x capacity" story by 12–18 months.
4
Chinese competition in global HVDC bids — margin pressure.
Chinese state-owned enterprises (TBEA, CHINT, etc.) have been aggressively bidding on global HVDC projects at significantly lower prices. In some markets (especially Middle East, Southeast Asia), they can undercut Quality Power by 20–30%. Quality Power's defence: (a) Western utilities prefer non-Chinese suppliers post-2020 geopolitical tensions, (b) FACTS and STATCOM require complex customisation that Chinese mass-production doesn't match well, (c) ABB/Siemens certifications and 100-country track record = trust premium. But this competition limits top-line growth potential in some geographies.
5
Multiple acquisitions simultaneously = management bandwidth risk.
In 18 months: Mehru (consolidated), Sukrut (50% JV), Nebeskie (26%), Turkey instrument transformer plant (evaluated). Running four integration tracks simultaneously while also executing a 9x Sangli capex program = significant management bandwidth risk. Any integration failure could be costly both financially and operationally. Mehru's "turned positive in first month" is encouraging — but Sukrut and multiple Turkey-related moves are still early stage.

High-voltage power equipment sector landscape — India and global
The global HVDC/FACTS market is a concentrated oligopoly. Globally, fewer than 10 companies manufacture 800kV HVDC-grade equipment. Barrier to entry: (1) Decades of engineering expertise, (2) High-voltage test labs worth ₹200–500 Cr, (3) Regulatory qualification processes (ABB/Siemens certify suppliers — takes 2–4 years), (4) Project execution track record (utilities don't risk HVDC infrastructure on new suppliers). Quality Power is the only Indian company that has cleared all these hurdles.
ABB India / Hitachi Energy — Global HVDC/FACTS market leaders
₹80,000–100,000 Cr mcap · India subsidiary of Swiss/Japanese MNCs · PE 60–80x
Dominant — different scale entirely
ABB/Hitachi Energy are the global #1 and #2 in HVDC technology. They build the entire HVDC system (pole design, converters, cable, transformers). Quality Power is a component supplier to these same companies (reactors, coils for ABB/Siemens projects). This means Quality Power both competes AND supplies to these giants. Revenue in India: ₹8,000–15,000 Cr. PE 60–80x. 3x from here is mathematically impossible. But Quality Power's relationship with ABB/Siemens as a qualified Indian supplier is a massive credibility signal.
TRIL (Transformers & Rectifiers India) — Best comparable Indian peer
₹7,929 Cr mcap · ₹2,395 Cr FY26 revenue · First HVDC repair order (PGCIL)
Good comparison — HVDC aspirant
TRIL just secured India's first HVDC transformer repair order from PGCIL. This is a qualification milestone — similar to what Quality Power achieved years ago. TRIL is at Layer 4 (standard power transformers up to 765kV), Quality Power is at Layer 1 (HVDC coils, FACTS). TRIL at ₹7,929 Cr mcap trades at 35x PE with FY26 revenue ₹2,395 Cr. Quality Power at ₹9,835 Cr mcap with ₹775 Cr TTM revenue = more expensive than TRIL on P/S basis, but justified by higher margins and HVDC moat. Not a direct replacement for each other.
