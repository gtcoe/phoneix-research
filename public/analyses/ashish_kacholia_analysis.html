🐋 The "Big Whale" Philosophy — 5 core principles Garvit must understand before evaluating his picks
1. Small & Mid-cap focus only: Kacholia invests almost exclusively in companies with ₹500–5,000 Cr market cap — exactly Garvit's sweet spot for 3x returns. He explicitly avoids large-cap blue chips as "growth is already priced in."

2. Early entry before institutional discovery: His edge is finding companies BEFORE sell-side coverage begins. When Kacholia files a >1% stake, the stock often jumps 10–20% on the day. His cost basis is typically 30–60% below current market price — so stocks in his portfolio now may already have his gain priced in.

3. Hold for 3–5 years minimum: Unlike traders, Kacholia holds through cycles. Many of his winners needed 2–3 years of patience (Shaily Engineering, Beta Drugs, Safari Industries). This patience is required — stocks he owns can be -30% before turning multibaggers.

4. Sector themes: Manufacturing, Make in India, Defence, Pharma, Niche services: His current portfolio is heavy in precision manufacturing (Balu Forge, Shaily Engineering), speciality chemicals (Fineotex, Tanfac), niche services (Knowledge Marine, NIIT Learning), and pharma (Beta Drugs, AMI Organics).

5. Governance above all: He exits when governance deteriorates. When promoters pledge shares aggressively or management changes, he sells quickly. Zero leverage on his own portfolio — he doesn't believe in borrowing to invest (relevant context for Garvit's 20L loan strategy).
