NBFC-specific KPIs — the right metrics for financial companies
For NBFCs, the correct valuation framework is Price/Book (P/B) and Return on Equity (ROE), not PE. The key metrics: AUM growth, Net Interest Margin (NIM), Credit cost, GNPA, ROA (Return on Assets), ROE (Return on Equity), D/E leverage, and CRISIL/ICRA credit rating (determines cost of borrowing). These replace the revenue/EBITDA metrics used for manufacturing companies.

SG Finserve unique advantage: supply chain GNPA is structurally near-zero
✓
Why supply chain finance has near-zero NPA structurally: The tripartite model (anchor corporation + dealer/distributor + SG Finserve) means SG Finserve lends to the dealers of large corporations (Tata, Bajaj, etc.). The anchor corporation's purchase orders are the collateral. If a dealer defaults, the anchor corporation's receivables cover the loan. This is why SG Finserve has zero GNPA — the credit risk is transferred to the anchor relationship, not to individual MSME creditworthiness. This is a fundamentally different risk profile from micro-finance or personal loans.

Business models — fundamentally different NBFC archetypes
SG Finserve — B2B Supply Chain Finance
1
The tripartite model (how it works): Large Indian conglomerate (Tata Motors, Bajaj Electricals, etc.) is the "anchor." Their dealers and distributors need working capital to buy inventory. SG Finserve provides this working capital — the anchor's purchase orders are implicit guarantee. The MSME dealer borrows ₹10–50 lakh for 30–90 days at 14–18% annual yield. SG Finserve funds this from banks at 8–10% → 600–800 bps NIM. At zero credit loss (anchor-backed), this is a near-perfect NBFC model. Gross disbursements reached ₹25,000 Cr in FY26.
2
The scale opportunity: Of India's top 500 corporations, barely 100 currently participate in supply chain finance programs. Every new large corporation that starts offering SCF to its dealer network becomes SG Finserve's potential customer. The total addressable market is estimated at ₹70–100 lakh Cr annually — SG Finserve's ₹4,000 Cr AUM is 0.004% penetration. Management guided: ₹10,000 Cr AUM in 3–4 years without fresh equity. That's 2.5x from today.
!
The technology differentiation: AI-driven credit monitoring, mobile app for channel partners, digital onboarding. Disbursement through WhatsApp (rare in NBFC). Processing fee income +297% YoY = the platform is monetising beyond just interest income. CRISIL AA is a critical moat — very few NBFCs at ₹3,742 Cr mcap have AA ratings. This enables borrowing from insurance companies and pension funds at rates unavailable to smaller NBFCs.
Emerald Finance — Multi-Product MSME NBFC
1
MSME direct lending (core): Loans to small businesses — typically ₹1–25 lakh, 12–36 month tenor, secured/unsecured. The Chandigarh-based company focuses on North India MSMEs with deep local credit knowledge. Historical NPA of 0.4–0.5% suggests excellent underwriting — this is extraordinarily low for MSME lending where 3–5% NPA is normal. The "promoter knowledge of creditworthiness" mentioned in company disclosures suggests relationship-based lending which the Aggarwal family has perfected.
2
Early Wage Access (EWA) — the growth engine: Emerald provides salary advance to employees of corporate clients before payday. The salary deduction mechanism means recovery risk is near-zero (employer deducts from next paycheck automatically). Gold loan syndication (₹375 Cr disbursed with ICICI and HDFC Bank as co-lenders) = asset-light high-fee income. This diversification is smart: different products have different risk profiles + different income recognition.
+
The CRISIL upgrade story (BBB from BB) — the most important catalyst: NBFC borrowing costs are a direct function of credit rating. BBB = can borrow from a wider set of lenders at lower rates. Before the upgrade, Emerald was paying higher rates. After upgrade, NIM expands even if revenue stays the same. The next upgrade to BBB+ or A- would be transformational — it would unlock institutional funding at 9% vs current 12–13%, dramatically improving profitability.

The broader NBFC lending landscape — where these two fit
Supply chain finance vs MSME direct lending — two distinct sub-sectors with different risk profiles
India's NBFC sector is deeply fragmented. SG Finserve operates in supply chain / channel finance — one of the safest NBFC models (near-zero NPA structurally, anchor-backed credit). Emerald Finance operates in MSME direct lending + financial wellness products — higher NPA risk but also higher yield (10–18% lending rates vs SG Finserve's 14–16%). The MSME lending market is far larger (₹6+ lakh Cr TAM) but also far more competitive. Emerald's differentiation is its EWA (salary advance) + gold loan syndication combo — these are relatively high-yield, secured products that compress NPA risk vs pure unsecured MSME lending. Emerald's model is smart — it combines high-yield product diversity with secured/near-secured recovery mechanisms.
