The STL story in 2025–2026 — a dramatic fall and a dramatic recovery
📉 The collapse (2021–2025): From ₹291 peak to ₹41 — a 94% fall in 4 years
Sterlite Technologies was a ₹15,000 Cr mcap company at its 2021 peak (₹291/share). Then a perfect storm hit:
• Global optical fiber oversupply: China produced massive fiber volumes, flooding global markets and crashing fiber prices from ~$12/km to ~$7–8/km. STL's margins, which were built on ₹10–12/km economics, collapsed.
• Market share decline: STL's global optical fiber market share (ex-China) fell from 12% to 8% as Chinese producers competed aggressively on price and quality.
• Services business demerger: STL demerged its global services/IT business (STL Networks → renamed Invenia, effective March 31, 2025). This simplified the company but removed a services revenue stream.
• High interest costs + debt: Aggressive capex for capacity expansion left STL with significant debt, and when margins collapsed, interest coverage fell dangerously.
• Full year FY25 loss — the worst operational year in company history.
Stock fell from ₹291 (Jan 2018 high, ₹200 in 2021) to ₹41.36 in April 2025 — a 94% fall from the all-time high.
📈 The recovery (FY26): 6 consecutive quarters of EBITDA margin improvement · +400% stock rally
The stock rallied from ₹41 to ₹295 (April 2026) — +614% in 12 months. The fundamental recovery is real:
• FY26 revenue ₹4,745 Cr (+18.8% YoY) — strongest annual growth in 3 years
• Q4 FY26 EBITDA ₹218 Cr (15.1% margin) — 6th consecutive quarter of sequential improvement. EBITDA margins recovering from ~7–8% trough toward management target of 18–20%
• Order book ₹5,325 Cr (1.1x annual revenue) — good near-term visibility
• North America at 36% of revenue — highest-margin market growing fast (BEAD program + data centers)
• AI data centre fiber demand — every GPU cluster requires massive fiber connectivity; STL is positioning as "AI-ready digital infrastructure"
• India-US trade deal tariff reduction (50% → 18% on telecom exports) — major competitive advantage for STL's US exports
• India's first hollow-core fiber (HCF) cable for data centres launched March 2026 — technology leadership signal
• PAT turning positive: ~₹56 Cr FY26 — first profitable year after FY25 losses

The critical question for investors: Is this a genuine structural recovery or a cyclical bounce that will reverse when fiber prices normalise? And is it too late to enter at ₹295 after a 614% rally from ₹41?

What STL makes and does — the two-division structure
After the March 2025 demerger of STL Networks (now Invenia), STL is now a focused optical networking products and solutions company. The demerger simplified the company — no more loss-making services drag. The remaining business is genuinely high-quality: STL is one of only 4–5 companies globally that can manufacture ultra-low-loss, high-count optical fiber cables at scale. This manufacturing scale is a genuine competitive advantage.
Optical Networking Business (ONB) — Core, ~95% of revenue
1
Optical fiber cable products (bulk): Standard single-mode fiber cables for telecom, FTTx, 5G networks. Sold by the kilometre to telcos, cable companies, government agencies worldwide. Revenue ₹1,174 Cr in Q3. This is the commodity base — high volume, margin sensitive to fiber pricing cycle. The China oversupply issue hit this hardest.
2
Optical connectivity products (Celesta IBR, patch cords): Higher-value, higher-margin products — pre-terminated assemblies, Celesta IBR enclosures (Industry's first high-density 1728F cabinet), patch cords for data centres. The "attach rate" (connectivity products as % of fiber revenue) = 17% currently vs 22% in FY25. Management target: 30%+. Each 1% improvement in attach rate = ~₹45 Cr additional revenue at current scale — at significantly higher margins.
3
Next-generation fiber (HCF, MCF, G.654.E): India's first hollow-core fiber cable for data centres launched March 2026. Multi-Core Fibre (MCF) successfully trialled with Colt Technology on London Metro. G.654.E ultra-low-loss fiber for long-haul. These premium products command 2–4x the price of standard fiber — and they're needed for AI data centres requiring sub-millisecond latency. This is where STL's 780-patent portfolio creates genuine IP differentiation.
+
AI/Data Centre (AIDC) portfolio: End-to-end fiber connectivity for AI clusters — from GPU-to-GPU intra-cluster connections (short reach, ultra-high density) to inter-data-centre long-haul links. Growing from 20% of revenue → management target 30%. Data centre clients pay premium pricing for certified, tested, low-latency fiber — this is highest-margin segment within ONB.
STL Digital (small, strategic) — ~5% of revenue
D
Enterprise IT services for communications/media: After demerger, STL Digital retained a smaller IT services practice (1,120 consultants) focused on BSS/OSS systems, SAP/Oracle ERP, cloud migration, and cybersecurity for telecom operators. Revenue contribution: small and stable (~₹50–70 Cr/year). March 2026: launched SecureNNov, an AI-driven cybersecurity portfolio — signals a move toward product-led offerings.
+
Why this matters strategically: STL Digital gives STL access to CIO/CTO relationships at telecom operators — the same people who buy optical fiber cables. Cross-sell potential (fiber + IT services) is real. This niche (communications-specific IT) is different from generic IT services — and the connection to STL's optical infrastructure heritage creates authentic domain expertise.
India + US + Europe — the three geographic pillars
US
North America (36% of YTD FY26 revenue) — the highest-margin market: US customers pay premium pricing for certified, US-made (or trusted-partner-made) fiber cables. The BEAD program ($42B) will fund fiber to 100M+ underserved homes — each home needs 200–400 meters of fiber = 20–40 billion fiber metres. STL's India manufacturing + export to US benefits enormously from the India-US trade agreement reducing tariffs from 50% to 18%. North America revenue growing from 20% to 36% — this geographic mix shift is the primary EBITDA margin recovery driver.
IN
India (35% of revenue) — BharatNet + commercial DC boom: BharatNet Phase 3 (connectivity for rural India) + private commercial data centres (Hiranandani, Adani, ADIA, NTT) driving Indian demand. India also benefits from anti-dumping measures on Chinese optical cable — STL as domestic manufacturer gets pricing protection. FTTx growth as Jio + Airtel + BSNL roll out fiber-to-home.
EU
Europe (28% of revenue) — 5G + FTTx rollout: European telcos rolling out FTTP (fiber to the premises) nationally. STL's Italy and South Africa plants give manufacturing proximity for European supply. MCF London trial with Colt positions STL for next-gen European DC orders.
Guidance analysis — management is cautiously optimistic on recovery timeline
STL management (Ankit Agarwal, MD) has been transparent about the recovery timeline and has maintained consistent guidance since Q2 FY26. The credibility of guidance improved significantly when Q4 FY26 EBITDA margins hit 15.1% — the first time since FY22 that margins exceeded the 15% threshold. The management re-appointment for 5 years signals board confidence in the recovery strategy.
✓ EBITDA margin recovery: 6 consecutive quarters of improvement — delivered
STL management guided "sequential EBITDA margin improvement" from Q2 FY25 onwards. Q4 FY26 EBITDA at 15.1% is the 6th consecutive sequential improvement — from ~8% trough to 15.1%. This is the single most important guidance delivery signal. Management target of 18–20% is now clearly in sight — the trajectory is confirmed. Revenue growth from BEAD (US) + BharatNet (India) + AI DC providing both volume and premium pricing that supports margin expansion.
Credibility: 9/10. Six consecutive quarters of improvement = track record, not just a promise.
✓ North America revenue growing: Guided to increase → delivered 36% of YTD FY26
Management guided increasing North America contribution (the highest-margin market) as BEAD demand materialises. YTD FY26 NA revenue at 36% confirms this — up from ~20% in prior year. Every percentage point shift toward North America is a margin improvement catalyst because US customers pay 30–50% premium over European/Asian customers for the same product.
Credibility: 9/10. North America ramp is real and quantified. BEAD execution from CY2026 will sustain this.
⚠ EBITDA target 18–20% — still 300–500 bps away as of Q4 FY26 (15.1%)
Management targets 18–20% EBITDA margins "in the medium term." Q4 FY26 at 15.1% is 290–490 bps below the target range. Achieving 18%+ requires: (1) Capacity utilisation above 70% (currently recovering from ~50–55%), (2) BEAD program scaling (lumpier than anticipated — state-by-state procurement), (3) AI DC connectivity products reaching 30% of revenue (from 20%), (4) Continued fiber price recovery (demand > supply rebalancing). Timeline: FY27–FY28 most likely for 18%+ margins.
Credibility: 7/10. Direction is right; the 18% target is achievable FY27–28 if demand catalysts execute as guided.
✗ Global market share (ex-China): 12% → 8% — significant competitive erosion
STL's global optical fiber market share (excluding Chinese producers) fell from 12% to 8% during the FY23–FY25 downturn. This was not fully telegraphed in advance and represents permanent competitive damage — customers who switched to other suppliers during STL's price/quality issues may not automatically return. Winning back market share requires sustained price competitiveness and product quality at scale. This is one metric where STL's guidance (of market share recovery) has not yet been validated.
Key metric to watch: Does STL market share stabilise at 8% or start recovering? FY27 order book composition will indicate.

Risks — the comprehensive view on a recovering large-cap
1
Prysmian $0.8 billion lawsuit — the most material risk.
Prysmian (Italy's largest cable company) has filed a $0.8 billion lawsuit against STL's US subsidiary. The dispute relates to alleged patent infringement or trade secrets. $0.8B vs STL's current ₹14,390 Cr (~$1.7B) market cap = the lawsuit represents ~47% of market cap if fully adverse. Management has said they are contesting and expect resolution, but an adverse verdict would be catastrophic for the balance sheet. This risk alone should command a significant discount to fair value — and current prices don't seem to fully account for it.
2
Market cap ₹14,390 Cr — wrong size for Garvit's 3x-in-3-years goal.
3x from ₹14,390 Cr = ₹43,170 Cr. At 25x PE, requires PAT of ₹1,727 Cr. FY26 PAT: ~₹56 Cr. Getting to ₹1,727 Cr requires 30x PAT growth in 3 years. Even optimistically: FY28 PAT ₹700 Cr at 25x PE = ₹17,500 Cr = 1.2x return. The math simply does not work for a 3-year 3x goal from current price at current market cap. This is the same problem we identified with PCBL and Skipper.
3
The stock has already rallied +400% from ₹41 — much of the recovery is priced in.
Anyone who bought STL at ₹41 (April 2025) and held to ₹295 (April 2026) already made 614% returns. At ₹295, the market cap (₹14,390 Cr) prices in a very optimistic FY27–28 recovery. If FY27 EBITDA reaches ₹900 Cr (at 15% margin on ₹6,000 Cr revenue), at 20x EBITDA = ₹18,000 Cr = 25% upside from current. That's not a 3x return — it's moderate upside from a dramatically recovered price.
4
BEAD program execution risk — state-by-state procurement is slower than expected.
The US BEAD program ($42B) has been slower to deploy than initially projected — NTIA (National Telecommunications and Information Administration) approval process, state matching requirements, and ISP participation requirements have delayed deployment from early-2025 expectations to late-2026. Each quarter of BEAD delay is a missed revenue quarter for STL's US operations. If BEAD takes until 2027–28 to show real volume, the FY27 revenue upside is deferred.
