CRITICAL: RPSG Ventures is a holding company — you must read it differently
Standard metrics (PE, revenue growth, PAT margin) are MEANINGLESS for RPSG Ventures. This is not a business — it is a collection of businesses. A negative consolidated PAT doesn't mean the company is doing badly. It means some subsidiaries are burning cash (Too Yumm FMCG) while others are generating excellent returns (Firstsource). The only correct way to value RPSG Ventures is Sum-of-Parts NAV Analysis — estimating the value of each subsidiary separately and then summing them up.
The holding company framework — how professionals value it
What you ADD UP (NAV = sum of parts)
1
Listed subsidiary stakes at market price: Firstsource Solutions (NSE: FSL) is publicly traded. RPSG holds ~53%. At FSL's current market price, this stake has a precise rupee value.
2
Unlisted subsidiaries at estimated value: LSG IPL franchise, Too Yumm, Quest Mall, Dr. Vaidya's, etc. — valued using comparable transactions, revenue multiples, or industry benchmarks.
3
Net debt adjustment: Subtract all consolidated debt, add cash holdings. RPSG Ventures has ₹1,155 Cr bank loans — reduces NAV.
!
Holding company discount applied: Indian holding cos trade at 20–50% discount to their intrinsic NAV. RPSG trades at ~80% discount — unusually steep.

Risks — what can keep the discount wide or widen it further
1
Too Yumm losses are open-ended — how long will it burn cash?
The consolidated loss in Q3 FY26 of ₹111 Cr is primarily from the FMCG brand investment. Too Yumm has been loss-making since 2017 — 8+ years. Without a clear path to profitability or a strategic sale, the market will continue pricing this as a liability. RPSG has not disclosed a timeline for Too Yumm turning EBITDA positive. If losses continue for another 3–5 years, it could absorb ₹1,000–2,000 Cr of capital that could have been distributed to RPSG Ventures shareholders.
2
Holding company discount could persist or widen — India has limited precedents for large discount narrowing.
Indian holding companies have historically maintained 30–60% discounts to NAV. RPSG's 80% discount, while extreme, may not narrow quickly. Without a catalyst (corporate restructuring, demerger, FMCG IPO, FSL stake sale), the discount simply persists. Investors looking for 3x in 3 years might find the timeline too uncertain. The IPL franchise is the wild card — its value won't be "realised" until there's a transaction (sale of partial stake, listing, or next broadcast rights cycle).
3
Firstsource growth dependent on US BPM market — Trump era policy risk.
Firstsource earns ~60–65% of its revenue from the US market (healthcare BPM primarily). Any healthcare reform, insurance market disruption, or "Buy American" IT/BPO outsourcing restrictions could impact FSL's top line. Additionally, AI automation (of which FSL is a beneficiary, not victim, per management) remains a structural risk to the traditional BPM model if its "UnBPO" differentiation fails to hold. FSL is the anchor — if FSL underperforms, the entire RPSG Ventures NAV shrinks.
4
LSG sports performance risk — no IPL title affects brand value and sponsorships.
LSG has never won an IPL title. It has missed playoffs in the last two seasons (2024, 2025). Poor on-field performance affects: (a) sponsorship renewal rates, (b) fan engagement, (c) BCCI's perception for renewal of franchise terms. While broadcast rights income is fixed, variable income from sponsorships and tickets depends on performance. Rishabh Pant's captaincy (bought for ₹27 Cr) is both a risk (what if he underperforms?) and an opportunity (if he leads LSG to title, franchise value jumps 20–30%).
5
Conglomerate complexity = permanent opacity discount.
With 30+ subsidiaries across IT services, FMCG brands, sports franchises in 3 countries, real estate, luxury fashion, and ayurveda — RPSG Ventures is one of India's most complex listed entities. No sell-side analyst covers it comprehensively. Institutional investors struggle to value it. This complexity itself creates a discount that may never fully close without significant corporate simplification. Every new acquisition (Manchester, FSP Design) adds to complexity rather than reducing it.
