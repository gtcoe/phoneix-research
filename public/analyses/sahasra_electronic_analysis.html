What Sahasra Electronic Solutions actually does — the business in full
💻 Sahasra ESDM — India's emerging integrated electronics + semiconductor company
Incorporated Feb 2023 (listed Oct 2024). Took over the running business of promoter Amrit Lal Manwani's proprietorship "M/s Sahasra Electronic Solutions" (started ~2000, ELCINA Export Prize 2022–23). Manufacturing in Noida (SEZ, 4 SMT lines) + Bhiwadi, Rajasthan (semiconductor packaging). CEO is Varun Manwani (second generation).

Two integrated businesses:
🔷 EMS Division (listed entity — SESL): Electronics System Design & Manufacturing from Noida SEZ. Products: PCB assemblies (automotive, medical, industrial, IT), box builds (laptops, tablets), LED lighting, IT accessories (motherboards, DRAM, SSDs, USB drives). Revenue model: contract manufacturing + design + sell. Certified: EN 9100:2018 (aerospace-grade quality), ISO 9001:2015. International exports to EU, UK, Middle East + domestic OEMs.

🔶 Semiconductor Division (Sahasra Semiconductors Pvt Ltd — 54.18% subsidiary): Bhiwadi, Rajasthan semiconductor packaging facility. Products: eSIMs, eMMC, micro-SD cards, COBs, COB-based USBs, LED driver ICs, BGA packages, NAND flash. Customers: telecom, consumer electronics, IoT, EV. SPECS subsidy ₹18.5 Cr received from govt. Phase-II ₹200 Cr capex planned (50% India Semiconductor Mission 2.0 grant expected).

Merger underway: SEPL + ITPL + SSSDPL merging into listed SESL (approved March 2026). Post-merger promoter rises to 73.77%. Simplifies governance, creates integrated semiconductor+EMS company.

The semiconductor angle — why Sahasra is different from pure EMS peers
🔬 India's semiconductor gap — ₹76,000 Cr mission

India imports 100% of its chips — domestic manufacturing is top policy priority

India's semiconductor import bill: $25+ Bn/year. The government's India Semiconductor Mission has committed ₹76,000 Cr to build domestic fabs. Micron (memory), TATA (fab), Sahasra (packaging) are all part of this ecosystem. Sahasra's Bhiwadi facility is one of the first domestic semiconductor packaging facilities — a critical link between chip design and finished product assembly. The SPECS subsidy (₹18.5 Cr received) validates government support.

📊 ESDM vs pure EMS — higher margin potential

Full-chain ESDM vs pure contract assembly — Sahasra has both

Pure EMS companies (Dixon, Amber) do final assembly at 5–8% EBITDA margins. ESDM companies add design + IP → 15–25% EBITDA. Sahasra targets this higher tier. In H1 FY26, they delivered 21.71% EBITDA margin — already in the premium ESDM tier, not the low-margin assembly tier that Dixon operates in. The in-house motherboard design and eSIM manufacturing confirm this higher-value positioning.
