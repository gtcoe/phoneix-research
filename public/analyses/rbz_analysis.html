Business model — a unique hybrid that Sky Gold does NOT have
RBZ is fundamentally different from Sky Gold. Sky Gold is a pure B2B manufacturer — it never sells to consumers. RBZ is a manufacturer that also runs its own jewellery stores under the "Harit Zaveri" brand. This B2C model gives RBZ higher margins but also means it competes with its own wholesale customers.

The antique jewellery moat — genuinely differentiated from Sky Gold
1
Jadau, Meena, Kundan, Polki artisan specialisation: RBZ makes traditional hand-crafted antique jewellery requiring generational artisan skill. This is NOT commoditisable by a machine. You cannot 3D print a Kundan piece. This creates a genuine artisan moat that Sky Gold's casting jewellery does not have.
2
Multi-generational promoter expertise: The Zaveri family has 2+ generations in Ahmedabad's antique jewellery trade. Customer relationships are personal, not transactional. You don't switch your Polki jewellery supplier easily.
3
Design portfolio in traditional jewellery is sticky: 951 new designs launched in Q3 FY26 alone. Traditional jewellery designs are tied to regional festivals, bridal ceremonies, and cultural occasions — demand is recurring and predictable.
4
Manufacturing cost advantage in B2C: Because RBZ manufactures in-house, its retail margins are higher than a pure retailer. The brand expense (marketing, store ops) is offset by the manufacturing savings vs buying from a third party.
!
Geographic concentration is the key weakness: Revenue is almost entirely from Gujarat (Ahmedabad retail + Gujarat-based wholesale customers). This is a different risk profile from Sky Gold's pan-India B2B supply chain.

PEG ratio 0.22x. This is even cheaper than Sky Gold's 0.45x. At 8x PE with 28%+ PAT growth, this is textbook "growth at a discount." The stock has de-rated because the market doesn't believe in the B2C transition. If the Surat and Rajkot stores deliver, this stock re-rates sharply.
Why is the stock cheap despite strong margins? Three reasons: (1) wholesale revenue is declining, making the headline growth look weak. (2) FY26 guidance was cut twice — market lost trust. (3) New stores are delayed — the catalyst investors were waiting for hasn't arrived yet. These are all fixable near-term issues, not permanent damage.
1
Wholesale revenue structurally declining — the most important risk.
Wholesale fell -12% in Q3 FY26, job work fell -46%. As RBZ opens its own retail stores in Gujarat (Surat, Rajkot), it is directly competing with its own wholesale clients. A manufacturer who becomes a retailer in the same geography creates channel conflict. Malabar and Kalyan store managers in Gujarat who buy from RBZ wholesale may reduce orders as they see RBZ opening stores nearby. If wholesale continues to decline and retail stores don't scale fast enough, revenue growth flatlines.
2
₹250 Cr inventory funding for new stores — balance sheet stress ahead.
Opening 4 flagship stores requires ~₹250 Cr of gold inventory. At current borrowing rates (~9-10%), this is ₹25 Cr annual interest cost — nearly half of FY25 PAT. If stores take longer than expected to ramp (12-18 months to maturity), the company faces high interest costs with delayed revenue. Current D/E was already rising (4.21x in FY24 vs 1.95x in FY23). Watch this carefully.
3
Guidance credibility damaged by two consecutive FY26 cuts.
First cut: ₹800 Cr → ₹700 Cr. Second cut: ₹700 Cr → ₹630–650 Cr. Any growth stock that cuts guidance twice in the same year takes a PE de-rating. Investors now discount RBZ's FY27 guidance of ₹1,000 Cr to ₹750–850 Cr in their models. Management needs at least 2 consecutive quarters of guidance delivery before trust rebuilds.
4
Geographic concentration — almost entirely Gujarat.
Retail revenue comes from one Ahmedabad store. New stores are in Surat and Rajkot — still Gujarat. Wholesale has wider reach (72 cities, 20 states) but the highest-value segment is Gujarat-concentrated. Any state-level regulatory issue, competitive entry by Malabar or Tanishq in Gujarat, or economic slowdown in Gujarat hits RBZ disproportionately.
5
B2C execution risk — RBZ is a manufacturer learning retail.
Running jewellery retail stores is fundamentally different from manufacturing. You need trained salespeople, store managers, visual merchandising, marketing spends, brand building, consumer loan tie-ups. RBZ has proven it can manufacture well. It has one store in Ahmedabad that works. But scaling to 4+ stores with ₹400 Cr each target revenue (management's long-term goal) requires building organizational capabilities it doesn't currently have at scale.
+
Mitigants:
Promoter holding 75% with zero pledge (best in the jewellery manufacturing peer group). CRISIL upgraded to BBB+/Positive — credit quality improving. Existing Ahmedabad store performing well. PAT growing 43% despite revenue growing only 14% — margin engine is genuinely working.

The one area where RBZ genuinely wins
Sky Gold's core vulnerability
Sky Gold's EBITDA margins are 7% and PAT margins 4%. For a ₹6,000 Cr revenue company, this is thin. Every working capital deterioration or interest rate increase hits PAT hard. The business is highly capital intensive and cash flow negative. Sky Gold is growing fast but isn't yet generating real cash.

RBZ's 12% EBITDA margin means it earns 70% MORE profit per rupee of revenue than Sky Gold. If Sky Gold can match RBZ's margins one day, the PAT story becomes extraordinary. But today, RBZ's business quality per rupee of revenue is superior.
Sky Gold's structural advantage
Sky Gold grows revenue at 45-75% CAGR vs RBZ's 15%. Sky Gold's addressable market (all organized jewellery across India) is 15x larger than RBZ's antique niche. Sky Gold is adding Reliance and Tanishq while RBZ watches its wholesale business decline.

Scalability is Sky Gold's dominant advantage. A ₹6,557 Cr mcap company growing at 75% will create far more absolute wealth than a ₹408 Cr company growing at 15%, even if the latter has better margins. In absolute return terms for Garvit's portfolio — Sky Gold wins decisively.
Sky Gold verdict — High conviction. Add ₹50,000 now. Core position.
Best-in-class revenue growth (76% YoY), management that beats every guidance, Tanishq+Reliance transformative client additions, Big 4 audit, promoter zero-salary alignment — everything you want in a core holding. The OCF negative is the only real risk, and it's actively improving. FY27 forward PE of 19-21x is genuinely cheap for this growth rate. From ₹344 entry, base case is 2-2.5x by FY28, bull case is 3x+. This is your highest conviction jewellery holding.
RBZ Jewellers verdict — Speculative position only. Small allocation ₹20,000–30,000 maximum.
RBZ has real merits: 12% EBITDA margins (best in class), 75% promoter holding (zero pledge), artisan antique jewellery moat, and extremely cheap valuation at 7-8x forward PE. But the business is in the middle of a difficult strategic transition from B2B to B2C, has cut FY26 guidance twice, faces declining wholesale revenue, and needs ₹250 Cr more debt to fund new stores. The upside is real (3x possible in bull case) but the execution risks are high. This is a speculative bet on a B2C transition, not a clear compounder.
Can you own both? Yes — but in the right ratio.
Both operate in the jewellery manufacturing space but serve different theses. Sky Gold = pure growth compounder (high volume, B2B, fast scale). RBZ = value + transition bet (cheap valuation, B2C optionality, artisan niche). Owning Sky Gold as core (₹85K+) and RBZ as speculative satellite (₹20–25K) gives you exposure to both the growth story and the value re-rating story without overweighting the riskier name.
